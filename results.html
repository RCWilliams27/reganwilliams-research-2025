Topological Structure

Explored how graphs encode shape and structure through algebraic tools like the Euler characteristic and Laplacians.

See Key Results
  • Computed Euler characteristic: \( \chi = |V| - |E| \)
  • Related Laplacian kernels to Betti numbers: \( b_0, b_1 \)
  • Verified discrete Morse inequalities: \( C_0 - C_1 = b_0 - b_1 = \chi \)

Entropy

Used von Neumann entropy to measure entropy across graph families and studied its behavior under gluing and disjoint union.

See Key Results
  • Computed entropy for paths, cycles, torus, stars, and more
  • Ranked graphs by entropy to reflect symmetry and connectivity
  • Studied gluing operations and their impact on entropy
  • Proved entropy additivity: \( S(\Gamma_1 \cup \Gamma_2) = S(\Gamma_1) + S(\Gamma_2) \)
